Waakye with Fish

A popular Ghanaian rice and beans dish served with fish, gari, boiled egg, and plantain.

Waakye Ingredients

  • • 2 cups rice
  • • 1 cup black-eyed peas
  • • 1 tsp salt
  • • Sorghum leaves
  • • Palm oil
  • • Fish (Tilapia or Red Snapper)
  • • Boiled eggs
  • • Gari (Cassava flakes)
  • • Fried plantain
  • • 1 large onion
  • • 3 fresh tomatoes
  • • Fresh pepper (Scotch bonnet)

Cooking Instructions

  1. Wash rice and beans thoroughly in clean water.
  2. Boil beans in a large pot until partially cooked (about 25 mins).
  3. Add sorghum leaves to the boiling beans to release their natural red color.
  4. Add the washed rice and salt. Stir gently.
  5. Simmer on low heat until both rice and beans are fully cooked and the water is absorbed.
  6. Prepare fish by seasoning with salt, ginger, and garlic, then fry until golden brown.
  7. Make a simple tomato sauce using blended tomatoes, onions, and pepper.
  8. Serve the Waakye hot, accompanied by the fried fish, a hard-boiled egg, fried plantain, and a sprinkle of gari.

Helpful Cooking Tips

💡 Soak beans overnight

This drastically reduces the cooking time and makes the beans easier to digest.

💡 Use fresh fish

Fresh tilapia or red snapper holds its shape better when fried and tastes superior.

💡 Serve hot

Waakye is best enjoyed immediately after cooking while the aromas are strongest.

💡 Avoid overcooking rice

Watch the water level carefully. You want the rice to be soft but not mushy.
